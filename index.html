Our mission

Language is roots.
Even far from home.

Ukrainian for You was born in 2025 from the pain of parents who saw their children gradually forgetting their mother tongue. We help children living abroad stay connected to their language, culture and identity — wherever they are.

Preserving cultural identity

Children remember who they are through language, songs, fairy tales and traditions of their people.

Building a community

Students find friends across Europe — peers who understand their journey and share the same roots.

Quality education without borders

Certified teachers, Ukrainian curriculum adapted for children growing up in a different language environment.

Learning formats

Online platform + offline school

Not every child has a computer. That's why we offer 4 flexible learning formats to suit every school and family situation.

Teacher's laptop + projector (offline)

The teacher uses the platform on their laptop connected to a projector or TV. Children interact verbally, work at the board, answer quizzes together. The teacher controls the platform — no devices needed for students.

School computer lab (hybrid)

If the school has computers or tablets — each child gets their own platform login. The teacher leads the lesson live while students complete interactive tasks individually.

Fully online (no school needed)

No Saturday school in your city? The child learns online from home with peers from across Europe. The live group lesson + self-study platform replaces the physical school entirely.

School has its own teacher

If the Saturday school already has a Ukrainian teacher, we provide the platform, curriculum, materials and methodological support. The teacher uses our content — saving hours of lesson preparation.

Iryna Lutsenko — founder, coach, Ukrainian language advocate

Ukrainian for You was born not in a boardroom, but from a real pain that Iryna witnessed firsthand. Travelling across Europe and speaking with Ukrainian families in Poland, Germany, Spain, France and the UK, she heard the same story again and again: children are forgetting Ukrainian. They switch to the local language, feel awkward speaking Ukrainian with their parents, and slowly lose the thread that connects them to their roots.

As a certified coach and educator, Iryna began collaborating with Ukrainian diaspora associations across Europe — in Warsaw, Berlin, Madrid, Paris, Prague and beyond. She discovered that dozens of Saturday schools existed but lacked a unified platform, structured curriculum and digital tools. The teachers worked on enthusiasm alone, without support.

In 2025, Iryna launched Ukrainian for You — a project that unites a digital platform, a community of teachers and a network of partner schools. Her coaching background shapes the project's philosophy: it's not about grammar drills, it's about children feeling proud to speak Ukrainian. About identity. About belonging.
